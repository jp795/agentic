THE LITTLE MERMAID
Author: Hans Christian Andersen
First Published: 1837 (in "Fairy Tales Told for Children, First Collection")

Summary:
Deep beneath the sea lives a young mermaid princess, the youngest of six
sisters, who is fascinated by the world above the waves. On her fifteenth
birthday she is allowed to surface and glimpses a handsome human prince
celebrating aboard a ship. A fierce storm sinks the vessel and she rescues
the drowning prince, leaving him on a shore to be found by others.

Consumed by love and longing for a human soul — which mermaids do not
possess — she visits the terrifying Sea Witch. The witch agrees to give
her legs in exchange for her beautiful voice, her most precious gift. There
is a terrible condition: if the prince marries another, the little mermaid
will dissolve into sea foam at dawn.

She goes to the prince as a mute young woman. He is enchanted by her grace
and beauty, but mistakes another woman for the one who rescued him and
announces his engagement. The little mermaid's heart breaks.

Her sisters offer her a knife — if she kills the prince before sunrise, she
can return to the sea and live. She cannot bring herself to do it. As dawn
breaks, she dissolves — but not into sea foam. Her selfless love transforms
her into a spirit of the air, given the chance to earn a soul through
centuries of good deeds. It is a bittersweet tale of love, sacrifice, and
the longing for something beyond one's own world.
