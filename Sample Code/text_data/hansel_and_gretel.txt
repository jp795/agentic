HANSEL AND GRETEL
Authors: Brothers Grimm (Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm)
First Published: 1812 (in "Kinder- und Hausmärchen", Volume 1)

Summary:
Hansel and Gretel are the young children of a poor woodcutter. When famine
strikes the land, their cruel stepmother convinces their father to abandon them
in the forest so that the adults might survive. Overhearing the plan, the clever
Hansel collects white pebbles and drops them along the path, allowing the
children to find their way home by moonlight.

The stepmother devises the plan again, and this time Hansel can only scatter
breadcrumbs — which are eaten by birds, leaving them truly lost. Deep in the
forest, the starving children discover a wonderful cottage made entirely of
gingerbread, candy, and sugar. As they begin to eat, a wicked witch emerges.
She lures them inside, then imprisons Hansel to fatten him up and forces
Gretel to work as her servant, intending to eat them both.

Gretel outsmarts the witch: when ordered to check the oven, she pretends not
to understand how, tricking the witch into demonstrating — then shoves her in
and slams the door. The children discover the witch's treasure, fill their
pockets with jewels, and find their way home. Their stepmother has died, their
father is overjoyed, and the family's poverty is ended by the witch's riches.
