SLEEPING BEAUTY
Author: Charles Perrault (earliest literary version)
First Published: 1697 (in "Histoires ou contes du temps passé")

Summary:
When a long-awaited princess is born to a king and queen, they hold a grand
celebration and invite the fairies of the kingdom to be her godmothers. Each
fairy bestows a magical gift upon the child — beauty, grace, wit, and more.
However, one old fairy who was not invited arrives in a fury and curses the
princess: she will prick her finger on a spindle and die.

A younger fairy softens the curse — instead of death, the princess will fall
into a deep sleep for one hundred years, to be awakened by a prince's kiss.
The king orders all spindles in the kingdom destroyed, but on her sixteenth
birthday the princess discovers a hidden tower, finds an old woman spinning,
and pricks her finger. She falls into an enchanted slumber, and the entire
castle falls asleep with her.

A century passes and the castle becomes hidden by a dense forest. A brave
prince hears the legend, fights through the thorns, and finds the sleeping
princess. He kisses her, breaking the spell. The princess awakens, the whole
castle stirs back to life, and the prince and princess are married, beginning
their happily ever after together.
