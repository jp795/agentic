LITTLE RED RIDING HOOD
Author: Charles Perrault (earliest literary version)
First Published: 1697 (in "Histoires ou contes du temps passé")

Summary:
A cheerful young girl, known by all as Little Red Riding Hood for the red
cloak she always wears, is sent by her mother through the forest to deliver
a basket of food to her sick grandmother. Her mother warns her to stay on
the path and not to stray.

In the forest, she encounters a cunning wolf. Rather than attacking her
directly, the wolf engages her in friendly conversation, learns where she
is going, and races ahead to grandmother's cottage by a shorter route.
The wolf devours the grandmother and disguises himself in her bed, waiting.

When Little Red Riding Hood arrives, she notices something unusual about
her "grandmother" — her big eyes, big ears, and big teeth. The wolf, unable
to contain himself, leaps from the bed and eats the girl too.

In the later and more widely known Brothers Grimm version (1812), the story
has a happier ending: a passing huntsman hears the wolf snoring, cuts open
its belly, and rescues both Little Red Riding Hood and her grandmother
unharmed. They fill the wolf's belly with stones, and he dies. Little Red
Riding Hood learns a valuable lesson about the dangers of straying from
the path and trusting strangers.
