CINDERELLA
Author: Charles Perrault (earliest literary version)
First Published: 1697 (in "Histoires ou contes du temps passé")

Summary:
Cinderella is a kind and gentle young woman who lives with her cruel stepmother
and two selfish stepsisters after the death of her father. Forced to work as a
servant in her own home, she endures their mistreatment with grace and patience.

When an invitation arrives for a grand royal ball, her stepsisters attend while
Cinderella is left behind. Her fairy godmother appears and, using magic, transforms
a pumpkin into a carriage, mice into horses, and Cinderella's rags into a beautiful
gown — complete with a pair of delicate glass slippers. She is warned that the
spell will break at midnight.

At the ball, the Prince is enchanted by the mysterious young woman and they dance
the night away. As the clock strikes midnight, Cinderella flees, losing one glass
slipper on the palace steps. The Prince searches the kingdom for the woman whose
foot fits the slipper. When he reaches Cinderella's home, the slipper fits her
perfectly. She is reunited with the Prince, they marry, and she lives happily
ever after — even forgiving her stepsisters for their cruelty.
